Using the weighted GPA calculator

  • This is NOT an official GPA calculator - it is intended for personal use only
  • Begin by selecting the duration of your undergraduate degree
  • Input the credit value and grade acheived for each paper, then click 'Add'
  • All years must have a total of 120 credits input before the weighted GPA can be calculated - the placeholder number in the credit value input field keeps track of the credit left to input
  • Click 'Calculate GPA' once the form has been completely filled
  • Clicking 'Reset' will remove all data that has been filled

How the weighted GPA is calculated

  • The best 120 credits/points scored each year are used in the calculation of the weighted GPA
  • A weighting factor ranging from 0.5 (first year of undergraduate degree) to 1.5 (final year of undergraduate degree) is assigned to each year of study - the increase of the weighting factor from one year to the next depends on the duration of the undergraduate degree (see below)
    • 3 year degree: 0.50, 1.00, 1.50 (most recent year last)
    • 4 year degree: 0.50, 0.80, 1.20, 1.50 (most recent year last)
    • 5 year degree: 0.50, 0.75, 1.00, 1.25, 1.50 (most recent year last)
  • Each paper is given a grade point value based on the 9-point scale
    • A+: 9
    • A: 8
    • A-: 7
    • B+: 6
    • B: 5
    • B-: 4
    • C+: 3
    • C: 2
    • C-: 1
    • fail: 0
  • The score for each paper is calculated as follows: weighting factor × credit value × paper grade
  • The sum of all paper scores is divided by: the duration of the undergraduate degree × 120
